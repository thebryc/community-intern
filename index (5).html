When Things Come Up

Situation Guide

⚠️
Angela is in the Philippines May 24 – June 8 (13 hours ahead of BR time). For anything urgent, text her — she checks messages in her morning, which is your late evening. For anything that can wait, leave a note and she'll respond when she wakes up.
Handle immediately
Within 24 hrs
Can wait / document
A social media post goes up with an error
▼
  1. Take the post down immediately if it has a factual error, misspelled name, or wrong date.
  2. Screenshot the error and your corrected version before deleting.
  3. Fix it and re-post. If the error was public-facing, add a brief correction note.
  4. Let Angela know what happened and how you handled it — even if fully resolved.
⚠️ Don't delete posts just because engagement is low — only if there's an actual error.
You don't know the answer to something a partner or prospect asks
▼
  1. Don't guess. Say: "That's a great question — let me find the right information and get back to you by [time]."
  2. Check the calendar and any planning docs in the shared drive first.
  3. If you still can't find it, text Angela with the specifics.
✅ A warm holding reply is always better than a wrong answer.
A prospective volunteer fills out the interest form
▼
  1. Log them in the volunteer CRM right away — name, contact info, org affiliation, date submitted.
  2. Send a warm follow-up within 24 hours to acknowledge receipt and outline the next step.
  3. Answer any questions you can from onboarding materials. Escalate anything you're unsure about to Angela.
  4. Track where they are in the pipeline: Interested → Intake → Onboarded.
✅ Prompt, personal outreach is what converts interest into actual volunteers.
A community partner or prospect contacts BRYC directly
▼
  1. Respond the same business day — even if just to acknowledge receipt.
  2. Thank them for reaching out and let them know someone will follow up shortly.
  3. Forward to Angela and note the date and contact name in the shared drive log.
  4. If they have an urgent program question, pull from program overview materials before replying.
✅ Partners notice how quickly you respond. First impressions matter here.
You're not sure which graduation post to put up and when
▼

The graduation post schedule is on the Calendar tab. Schools are grouped by graduation date — post on or just after that date.

  1. Use the graduation post template (in the shared folder with HS logos).
  2. Double-check the school name spelling before posting.
  3. If a school isn't on the list but you have their graduation info, flag it to Angela before posting separately.
⚠️ Don't combine schools into one post — each school's community wants to see their name featured.
A volunteer prospect goes quiet after initial interest
▼
  1. Wait 5–7 business days after your last outreach before following up.
  2. Send one warm, brief check-in: "Hi [Name] — just following up to see if you have any questions about volunteering with BRYC. We'd love to have you!"
  3. If no response after a second follow-up, mark them as cold in the CRM and move on.
  4. Log all attempts and dates in the CRM so there's a clear record.
You're not sure what to prioritize on a given day
▼
  1. Check the Calendar tab for anything dated today or the next 2–3 days.
  2. Hard-dated tasks (graduation posts, event setup, deadlines) come before open-ended work.
  3. When in doubt: external-facing posts and outreach → volunteer pipeline → internal admin.
  4. If you've handled everything time-sensitive, ask Angela what to move forward on next.
You need to prep or distribute volunteer welcome kits
▼
  1. Confirm the kit contents with Angela before assembling — the list may change each season.
  2. Check inventory against the list. Flag anything that needs to be ordered with enough lead time.
  3. Kits go out when volunteers complete intake and are confirmed — not before.
  4. Track distributions in the CRM or volunteer tracker so nothing gets sent twice.
You're asked to help with a physical task or space setup
▼

This is a normal part of the role — setup, handiwork, space prep, and event logistics all fall under your responsibilities when the team needs it.

  1. Get clear on what's needed, where, and by when before you start.
  2. For larger setups (Orientation, training rooms), ask for a checklist or walkthrough so nothing gets missed.
  3. If something looks unsafe or beyond your ability to handle alone, say so — always okay to flag that.
